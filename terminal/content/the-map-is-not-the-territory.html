the map is not the territory

a mental model from Alfred Korzybski (1931): our representations of reality — maps, models, frameworks, other people's systems — are always abstractions, never the thing itself.

the idea

maps are useful. they help us navigate. but they leave things out. they simplify. they were drawn by someone else, for their purposes, at a specific time.

the territory is what's actually there. messy, complex, alive.

the danger: confusing the map for the territory. following someone else's system as if it is the path, rather than a path.

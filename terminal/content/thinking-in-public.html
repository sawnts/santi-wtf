thinking in public

sharing ideas before they're finished seems counterintuitive, but this is what a digital garden is for — not polished essays, but thoughts in progress.

why would anyone in their right mind do that?

accountability — when ideas are public, you have to tend them. the garden creates healthy pressure to develop your thinking.

feedback — other people see things you miss. sometimes a stranger's comment can trigger fresher perspectives.

connection — you find your people by showing your work. people are naturally attracted to people who share their interests and curiosities.

compounding — ideas build on ideas. linking is thinking. a public archive of your thinking becomes a resource you — and others — can draw from forever.

the fear is real

what if it's wrong? what if it's dumb? what if someone disagrees?

that's the point. the map is not the territory — your ideas are maps, not absolute truths. getting them out of your head and into the world is how you refine them.

a digital garden isn't a stage. you're not here to perform. you're here to share what you're thinking without pressure to be right. dance like no one's watching.

borrowing ideas from one field and applying them in another.

this is [integration](/notes/integration/) in practice. the best ideas often come from collisions between domains that don't usually touch.

## examples
**[david ogilvy](/notes/david-ogilvy/)** — chef, salesman, spy, farmer, then ad man. each career fed the next. he understood products because he sold stoves door-to-door. he understood research because he worked at gallup. he understood persuasion because he was a spy.

**[miles davis](/notes/miles-davis/)** — didn't just play jazz. pulled in classical harmonies, rock energy, electronic textures. he invented new genres by refusing to stay in one.

**steve jobs**— his calligraphy class influenced typography in the mac. his interest in buddhism influenced his design philosophy — simplicity and minimalism.

## how to practice it
- read outside your field
- talk to people who do different work
- ask "how would X approach this?"
- notice patterns that repeat across domains

[the map is not the territory](/notes/the-map-is-not-the-territory/), but maps from different territories can reveal surprising overlaps.

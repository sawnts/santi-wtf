the internet used to feel like exploring someone's brain.

not their brand. not their feed. their actual brain — laid out in html, links intentionally arranged, background colors carefully selected. you'd stumble onto someone's homepage and feel like you'd found something truly special.

that internet is gone. i want it back.

## why i built this garden
a [digital garden](/notes/digital-garden/) is a collection of notes that grow over time. unlike a blog, where posts are polished and sorted by date, a garden is always evolving. ideas connect to each other. old thoughts get updated when new context arrives. nothing is really ever "done."

it's less about publishing and more about [thinking in public](/notes/thinking-in-public/).

that scares me. sharing half-formed thoughts feels vulnerable in a way that polished essays don't. what if it's wrong? what if it's dumb? what if someone reads it and realizes i'm as lost as anyone?

but i think that's the point.

most of what i write never sees the light of day. it sits in folders, collecting dust, slowly becoming irrelevant. this garden is my attempt to change that — to create pressure to develop my thinking, and maybe find people who are into the same weird shit i am.

the best ideas often come from combining concepts that don't obviously belong together. 

[ogilvy](/notes/david-ogilvy/)'s life is a masterclass of creative cross-pollination. the legendary ad-man cooked at the hotel majestic in paris, sold stoves door-to-door, did research for Gallup, worked as a british intelligence officer, and even farmed tobacco — all before founding his famous ad agency at 38. this eclectic background gave him a deeper understanding of both products and consumers.

## the opposite of splitting
while [splitting](/notes/splitting/) is about breaking things down to understand them, integration is about reassembling them in new ways. both skills are essential.

[miles davis](/notes/miles-davis/) didn't just play jazz — he integrated classical harmonies, rock rhythms, and electronic textures. the result was something entirely new.

## how to practice integration
- read widely outside your field
- look for structural similarities between different domains
- ask "what would x think about y?"
- combine tools and techniques from unrelated disciplines

> "creativity is just connecting things. when you ask creative people how they did something, they feel a little guilty because they didn't really do it, they just saw something." 
> — steve jobs

before you can understand something, you often need to break it apart. this is splitting — the analytical complement to [integration](/notes/integration/).

## when to split
splitting is useful when:

- a problem feels overwhelming
- you can't identify the root cause
- you need to explain something complex
- you want to improve a process

## techniques
**first principles** — break down to fundamental truths and rebuild from there.

**5 whys** — keep asking "why" to drill down to root causes.

## the danger
pure analysis can miss emergent properties. a song is more than the sum of its notes. after splitting, remember to step back and see the whole again.

[ogilvy](/notes/david-ogilvy/) was a master at both: he could dissect what made ads work, then synthesize that knowledge into new creative (see [integration](/notes/integration/)).

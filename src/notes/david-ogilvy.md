the father of advertising. insisted on research before creativity.

chef at the hotel majestic in paris, door-to-door stove salesman, gallup researcher, british intelligence officer, farmer — all before founding an ad agency at 38. He credited this eclectic background with giving him a deeper understanding of both products and consumers.  no wonder he sold billions of dollars for some of the biggest brands in the world with his damn pen.

a visionary with a deep understanding of [integration](/notes/integration/). in an interview, he said: "i don't think the world should be divided into creative people and the others... i think copywriters should become, have a few years as an account executive, and then you can move account executives into copy so that they become generalists." 

> "the consumer isn't a moron; she is your wife."
> — david ogilvy
master of [integration](/notes/integration/) and [splitting](/notes/splitting/).

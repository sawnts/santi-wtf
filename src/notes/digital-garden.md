this is a digital garden — a place for ideas to grow over time.

## why a garden?
blogs are streams — newest first, soon forgotten. gardens are different. notes evolve. old ideas connect to new ones. the structure grows organically.

it's less about *publishing* and more about *thinking in public*.

i originally got the idea to create a digital garden after stumbling into andy matuschak's [working notes](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/About_these_noteshttps://notes.andymatuschak.org/About_these_notes) a couple years back.

but i digress...

## the basics
notes live as simple text files on my computer. i write them in [obsidian](https://obsidian.md). when i'm ready to share, a simple script converts them to web pages.

## how ideas connect
[linking is thinking](/notes/linking-is-thinking/). in my notes, i can link to other notes using double brackets.

 *backlinks* are also crucial. when note A links to note B, note B automatically knows about it and shows "what links here" at the bottom. this linking matters because:

- ideas don't exist in isolation — they gain meaning through connections
- i can stumble upon relationships i forgot about
- i can trace how concepts relate to each other

it's like a personal wikipedia, but messier and a bit more honest.

## how ideas evolve
not all ideas in the garden are equal.

- 🌱 **seedling** — rough thoughts, barely formed
- 🌿 **growing** — taking shape, needs tending
- 🌲 **evergreen** — solid, stable ideas

this helps me (and you) know what's half-baked vs well-developed.

if you're interested in starting your own digital garden, [here's a great how-to piece](https://nesslabs.com/author/annelaure) by anne-laure le cunff.

integration

the best ideas often come from combining concepts that don't obviously belong together. ogilvy called this "creative cross-pollination."

the opposite of splitting

while splitting is about breaking things down to understand them, integration is about reassembling them in new ways. both skills are essential.

miles davis didn't just play jazz — he integrated classical harmonies, rock rhythms, and electronic textures. the result was something entirely new.

how to practice integration

  • read widely outside your field
  • look for structural similarities between different domains
  • ask "what would x think about y?"
  • combine tools and techniques from unrelated disciplines

"creativity is just connecting things. when you ask creative people how they did something, they feel a little guilty because they didn't really do it, they just saw something." — steve jobs

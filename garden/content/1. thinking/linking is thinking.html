linking is thinking

good ideas don't live in isolation. they live in relationship.

linking notes is the act of drawing explicit connections between ideas. not filing — connecting. a note isn't valuable because it's stored somewhere fancy. it's valuable because it touches other notes in meaningful ways.

your digital garden isn't a filing cabinet. it's a web. the nodes are ideas, the edges are links, and the structure is your thinking visualized.

why

creativity is combinatorial. new ideas emerge from the cross-pollination of existing ones. the more connections you make, the more surface area for insight.

linking also forces clarity. you can't connect an idea you don't understand. the act of asking "what does this relate to?" sharpens the thought.

over time, your web becomes a thinking partner. you stop searching for ideas and start traversing them. paths emerge that you didn't plan. that's the payoff.

how

  1. link as you write — don't wait. when a related idea comes to mind, link it immediately.
  2. link to ideas, not categories — perfectionism kills creativity is a lot more useful than creativity.
  3. link bidirectionally — if A relates to B, make sure you can find A from B.
  4. link with intention — ask why the connection matters. making effective connections
  5. revisit and relink — your best connections come from rereading old notes with new context.

the goal isn't to link everything. it's to link meaningfully. quality over quantity. every link should earn its place.

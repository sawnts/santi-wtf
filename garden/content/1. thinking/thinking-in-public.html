thinking in public

sharing ideas before they're finished.

this is what a digital-garden is for. not polished essays, but thoughts in progress. seedlings alongside evergreens.

why do it

accountability — when ideas are public, you have to tend them. the garden creates pressure to develop your thinking.

feedback — other people see things you miss. sometimes a stranger's comment unlocks a stuck idea.

connection — you find your people by showing your work. the weird specific thing you're into? someone else is too.

compounding — ideas build on ideas. linking is thinking. a public archive of your thinking becomes a resource you can draw from forever.

the fear

what if it's wrong? what if it's dumb? what if someone disagrees?

that's the point. the map is not the territory — your ideas are maps, not truth. getting them out of your head and into the world is how you refine them.

the soft guy era question: what if you just... didn't perform? what if you just shared what you're thinking, without pressure to be right?
